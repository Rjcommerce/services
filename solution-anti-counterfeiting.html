Real-world risks

The Cost of Not Knowing

These aren't customer complaints. They're the moments a single fake becomes a legal, financial, or reputational event.

"A customer was hurt by a counterfeit sold under your name. Whose liability is that?"
Courts and headlines don't distinguish between "our product" and "a fake wearing our label" as fast as your legal team needs them to — the lawsuit names your brand first.
"Customs just seized a shipment. Is it yours, or a copy good enough to fool your own team?"
Without a way to verify at the point of seizure, a legitimate shipment can be held for weeks while a real counterfeit slips through somewhere else.
"A viral post shows your product failing — except it's not your product."
By the time your team identifies it as counterfeit, the damage to trust has already spread further than any correction will reach.
"Your biggest distributor is quietly buying from someone else, because fakes come in 20% cheaper."
When a knockoff undercuts your price convincingly enough, the channel itself starts choosing fake over loyalty.

Three Layers — One Framework, No Gaps

Most anti-counterfeiting stops at one method. A single method is exactly what a counterfeiter needs to learn to copy.

Overt Verification

Holographic Security Label

Overt protection can take many accepted forms — holograms, color-shifting inks, tamper-evident seals. Smart Epsilon uses a tamper-evident, nano-OVD holographic label anyone can verify with the naked eye, no device required, before a single scan happens.

Instant naked-eye validation Tamper-evident seal
Point-of-sale visual check before scanning happens.

Covert Detection

Geo-Tagged Clone Detection

Covert protection can take many accepted forms — UV inks, embedded watermarks, encrypted codes. Smart Epsilon uses geo-tagged clone detection — every genuine scan is logged by location, and the moment the same code is scanned somewhere it shouldn't be, it's flagged automatically.

Real-time duplicate scan alert Geolocation & timestamp log
Live global geolocation tracking & instant duplicate flags.

Forensic Authentication

AI Watermark Authentication

An invisible marker embedded at the point of print, readable only by Smart Epsilon's verification system — the layer a counterfeiter doesn't even know to look for.

Micro-pattern print signature AI texture scanner
Micro-pattern watermark isolated under optical magnification.

Before you ask

Frequently Asked Questions

Can holograms be copied?
Basic holograms can be replicated with enough effort — that's exactly why ours is one of three layers, not the whole defense. Even if the overt layer were ever convincingly copied, covert geo-detection and forensic watermarking still catch it.
Do we need new packaging or printing hardware?
No — the label integrates into existing packaging workflows, and the watermark embeds at the point of print without new equipment or line slowdowns.
What happens when a clone is detected?
An alert fires immediately to your brand protection team with the exact GPS location and timestamp — not a monthly report.
How fast can we integrate Smart Epsilon with our SAP or Oracle ERP?
Integration takes ~2 weeks using native REST APIs and GS1 EPCIS connectors. No changes are required to your existing production line database schema or SAP S/4HANA core.
What is the cost structure per serialized unit?
Tiered volume pricing starting at fractions of a cent per unit with zero upfront capital equipment expenditure.
Can regional field sales reps use their personal smartphones for inspection?
Yes, the Smart Epsilon inspection app runs natively on both iOS and Android smartphones, featuring offline scan caching for remote field and warehouse areas.
