Real-world risks

The Cost of Not Knowing

These aren't customer complaints. They're the moments a single fake becomes a legal, financial, or reputational event.

01
A customer was hurt by a counterfeit sold under your name. Whose liability is that?
Courts and headlines don't distinguish between "our product" and "a fake wearing our label" as fast as your legal team needs them to. The lawsuit names your brand first.
02
Customs just seized a shipment. Is it yours, or a copy good enough to fool your own team?
Without a way to verify at the point of seizure, a legitimate shipment can be held for weeks while a real counterfeit slips through somewhere else.
03
A viral post shows your product failing, except it's not your product.
By the time your team identifies it as counterfeit, the damage to trust has already spread further than any correction will reach.
04
Your biggest distributor is quietly buying from someone else, because the fakes come in 20% cheaper.
When a knockoff undercuts your price convincingly enough, the channel itself starts choosing fake over loyalty.

Three Layers — One Framework, No Gaps

Most anti-counterfeiting stops at one method.

A single method is exactly what a counterfeiter needs to learn to copy.

QR-based consumer verification sits on top of all three: a fast, familiar way for anyone to confirm authenticity in seconds, backed by layers a counterfeiter can't see or replicate.
Tamper-Evident / Nano-OVD

Holographic Security Label

Overt protection can take many accepted forms: holograms, color-shifting inks, tamper-evident seals. Smart Epsilon uses a tamper-evident, nano-OVD holographic label anyone can verify with the naked eye, no device required, before a single scan happens.

Instant naked-eye validation Tamper-evident seal No device required
Live Geo-Flagging

Geo-Tagged Clone Detection

Covert protection can take many accepted forms: UV inks, embedded watermarks, encrypted codes. Smart Epsilon uses geo-tagged clone detection. Every genuine scan is logged by location, and the moment the same code is scanned somewhere it shouldn't be, it's flagged automatically.

Real-time duplicate alert Geolocation & timestamp log Instant brand protection team notification
Invisible Layer

AI Watermark Authentication

An invisible marker embedded at the point of print, readable only by Smart Epsilon's verification system. It's the layer a counterfeiter doesn't even know to look for.

Micro-pattern print signature AI texture scanner Impossible to copy without access

No Matter Who's Asking,
the Answer Is the Same.

The question changes depending on who's asking. The answer doesn't.

Can a consumer actually tell the difference without our help?

Yes. The holographic label is visible before any scan, and the QR confirms it in seconds after.

What stops someone from photographing and reusing a genuine code?

Geo-tagged detection catches a code being scanned somewhere it shouldn't be, the moment it happens. Not weeks later in a report.

Could a sophisticated counterfeiter eventually copy the label itself?

That's exactly why there are three layers, not one. Forensic authentication doesn't rely on anything visible enough to copy.
